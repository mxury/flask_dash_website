PhD Research & Publications

Technological advances are closely interlinked with our knowledge of the properties of materials and how they change under a variety of external parameters, e.g. temperature. In solid materials (i.e. not liquid/gas) electrons play a major role in determining the phase and characteristics like conductivity.

During my PhD my research concentrated on investigating the phase transitions of semimetals, i.e. materials that are an in-between phase of electrical insulators and conductors. I constructed lattice models and included a variety of interactions, and then from a statistical point of view considered their evolution upon increasing interaction strength. The fruits of that 4-year long research can be found below in the shape of three peer-reviewed papers.

Interplay of interactions and disorder at the charge density wave transition of two-dimensional Dirac semimetals

Using Renormalisation Group techniques we consider the effects of weak fermionic disorder on the quantum phase transition between a Dirac semimetal and a charge density wave insulator in two-spatial dimensions. It was found that the interacting fixed point is stable against weak disorder but transitions into a glassy phase in the strong-disorder region.

Published in Physical Review B, 105, 075143 (2022)

Fermionic criticality of anisotropic nodal point semimetals away from the upper critical dimension: Exact exponents to leading order in 1/N

We show with the use of a soft cutoff formulation of RG that it is vital to consider the effect of order parameter screening in gapless fermionic systems that are subject to strong short-ranged interactions. We then compute the exact critical exponents for the phase transition between nodal point semimetals and charge density wave insulators.

Published in Physical Review Research, 2, 043265 (2020)

Quantum criticality of semi-Dirac fermions in 2+1 dimensions

Semi-Dirac fermions are quasiparticles that are found at the topological phase transition between a Dirac semimetal and a trivial band insulator, where they disperse linearly in one momentum direction and quadratically in the other. We consider the quantum phase transition between such a semi-Dirac semimetal and various gapped phases using Renormalisation Group techniques.

Published in Physical Review B, 100, 155101 (2019)
